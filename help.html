Recycle

Make recycling a daily habit.Try to recycle all the packaging that comes with product you purchase.Purchase items with minimal and recycable packaging.Don't throw electronics out, for facilities exist that allow you to dispose of them.

Grow Plants and Trees

Consider growing a garden and grow plants that require minimal watering and attention, or plant trees.Planting a tree will soak up carbon from the atmosphere and provide shade.

Use Highly Efficient Appliances

If you are replacing your appliances look to purchase appliances with energy star ratings. These ratings are the international standard for energy efficient consumer products and utilize a minimum of 20% energy, which means savings for you and the environment.

Conserve Water

Save water by taking less time in the shower, and make sure to turn the water off when shaving or brushing your teeth. Treating and transporting water requires energy, while water conservation results in reduced energy requirements and carbon emissions.

Drive Less

Try to walk and bike as much as possible, not only is this better for the environment but it is better for your overall health. You should also take public transit systems or carpool whenever you can.

Repurpose

If you have any clothing or household goods that aren't of any use, give them new purposes. Any used clothing can be donated to charity or can be given to friends or family. Use your old shirts as rags for cleaning. The amount of waste being sent to landfill sites is reduced when you repurpose your unused materials, there is no need to use energy for recycling, and others can benefit from your used items.
